{% trans "What is a Tango Strategy?" %}

{% blocktrans %} The Tango strategy is a dynamic market response system tailored to capitalize on both upward and downward price movements. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}

Here's how it works:

Market Direction Determination: The strategy first gauges the market's potential direction. It does this by setting two trigger prices, equidistant from the current market price: one above and one below. The distance from the current price is determined as a certain percentage of the primary price.

Position Entry: The Tango strategy patiently waits until the market price reaches either of these trigger points. When the lower trigger price is hit, a short position is initiated, predicting a potential decline. Conversely, when the higher price is reached, the strategy goes long, anticipating a surge.

Profit and Risk Management: After entering a position, the strategy implements a dual mechanism for both profit-taking and risk management. A take-profit and a stop-loss are set, each spaced at the same interval as the initial trigger points.

Re-entry After Profit: In the event of the take-profit being hit, the strategy takes this as a confirmation of its market prediction and re-enters the position in the same direction. This cycle repeats until a stop-loss is triggered.

Position Reversal: If a stop-loss is activated, indicating a possible misjudgment of the market's direction, the Tango strategy swiftly changes tack. It enters a position in the opposite direction, adjusting to the new market sentiment.

In essence, the Tango strategy is a continuous loop of making informed predictions, reaping profits, and efficiently managing risks. Its beauty lies in its adaptability, always ready to make the most out of whichever direction the market sways.
